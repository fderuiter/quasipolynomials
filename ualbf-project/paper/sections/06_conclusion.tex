\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The Unified Algebraic-Lattice Bipartition Framework (UALBF) provides a
rigorous, computationally tractable methodology for analysing the
quasiperfect number problem.  By formalising parity constraints, the
modulo-8 obstruction, exact valuation divisibility, the abundancy index
ceiling $H(N) < N/\varphi(N)$, the totient ratio decomposition
$N/\varphi(N) = H(N) \cdot C$, and two independent correction factor
bounds---$C < 1.022$ via head--tail product splitting (in
\texttt{Abundancy.lean}) and $C < 36/35$ via a pure-$\mathbb{Q}$
telescoping-sum approach (in \texttt{CorrectionFactor.lean})---within the
Lean~4 theorem prover, we have established a mechanically verified
foundation for aggressively pruning the search space.  The formally
certified bound $N/\varphi(N) < 2.4675$ for QPNs coprime to~15
(\cref{thm:totient-bound}), combined with the Prasad--Sunitha bound
$\omega(N) \ge 15$ (\cref{thm:prasad-sunitha}), the cyclotomic
decomposition of $\sigma(p^{2e})$ (\cref{lem:cyclotomic-expansion}),
and the formal exhaustion certificate
\texttt{no\_\allowbreak{}solution\_\allowbreak{}no\_\allowbreak{}qpn}
(\cref{thm:no-sol-no-qpn}), provides multiple independent avenues for
branch elimination.

The integration of this formal core with a highly concurrent, lock-free
Rust engine---employing Z3-backed CDCL conflict learning, LLL lattice
basis reduction, cyclotomic sieve decomposition, Yamada tail starvation
analysis, and $\mathcal{O}(1)$ ray-cast sieving---has definitively
pushed the lower bound for quasiperfect numbers to $N > 10^{37}$, with
an empirically verified zero-panic telemetry.

The success of the Lean/Rust hybrid architecture highlights a powerful
paradigm for future mathematical investigations: bridging the gap
between absolute, mechanically checked formal truth and raw, parallel
computational power.  This model eliminates the ``verification gap''
inherent in unverified heuristic programming, ensuring that
optimisation artifacts or integer overflows cannot compromise the
integrity of mathematical bounds.

Future work will focus on four fronts:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Completing the Remaining Zsigmondy Stubs:}
    The decomposed proof of Zsigmondy's theorem is now almost
    entirely formalised (see~\cref{subsec:cyclotomic_zsigmondy}).
    Only two narrow \texttt{sorry} stubs remain: the coprime
    induction case inside \texttt{primeFactors\_\allowbreak{}bound\_\allowbreak{}all}
    (needed to bound the proper-divisor product of $\Phi_n(2)/n$)
    and the wrapping lemma
    \texttt{cyclotomic\_\allowbreak{}eval\_\allowbreak{}two\_\allowbreak{}gt\_\allowbreak{}index}.
    Closing these would eliminate all remaining \texttt{sorry}
    obligations in \texttt{Cyclotomic.lean} and complete the fully
    verified Zsigmondy path.  This is the immediate next step.

  \item \textbf{Deep Formalisation of Divisibility Chains:}
    Formalising generalised modulo-3 and modulo-9 constraints in
    Lean~4 to further restrict permissible suffix structures for
    candidates divisible by~3, where the Prasad--Sunitha bound does
    not apply.

  \item \textbf{Distributed Scaling:}
    Expanding the Rust engine's architecture to a distributed cluster,
    allowing distinct algebraic ``cubes'' to be assigned dynamically
    across hundreds of nodes.

  \item \textbf{Enhanced Lattice Pruning:}
    Augmenting the LLL Diophantine sieving with BKZ reduction and
    tighter tolerance analysis to prune starvation branches at
    shallower depths.
\end{enumerate}

By continuing to iteratively tighten the formally verified bounds and
parallelising the remaining computational search space, we aim to
definitively resolve the quasiperfect number hypothesis.
