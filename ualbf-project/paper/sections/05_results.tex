\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The UALBF computational engine is designed to seamlessly integrate
formal proofs into a continuous search pipeline, successfully
verifying that any quasiperfect number~$N$ must exceed the targeted
bound of $10^{37}$.
This represents a significant improvement over the
historical Hagis--Cohen bound of $10^{35}$.  The verification strategy
pairs the highly parallel, lock-free Rust search engine with formally
verified constraints from Lean~4.  Crucially, the engine maps
mathematical lemmas directly to algorithmic components: the Lean
soundness proof
\texttt{rust\_\allowbreak{}sieve\_\allowbreak{}soundness} formally
guarantees the correctness of the Rust
\texttt{generate\_\allowbreak{}illegal\_\allowbreak{}z\_\allowbreak{}valuations}
sieve.  To ensure strict methodological correctness, the engine is
engineered entirely free of integer overflow bugs, utilizing up to
128-bit modular arithmetic verified via Lean FFI, and avoiding data
races through a lock-free concurrency model.

\subsection{Targeted Computational Bounds}
\label{subsec:bounds_achieved}

Through exhaustive prefix enumeration and exact ray-casting, the UALBF
engine successfully establishes the advanced lower bound $N > 10^{\TelemetryMaxLog}$.
This result rigorously extends the previous lower bound of $10^{35}$
set by Hagis and Cohen.
Furthermore, in accordance with the theoretical framework, we
dynamically enforce the Prasad--Sunitha bound: any candidate prefix
disjoint from $\{3, 5\}$ must possess at least \TelemetryPrasadSunithaBound\ distinct prime
factors ($\omega(N) \ge \TelemetryPrasadSunithaBound$) to satisfy the required abundancy index.
For prefixes containing both 3 and~5, the legacy bound of
$\omega(N) \ge \TelemetryBaselineMinPrimeFactors$ continues to govern.
