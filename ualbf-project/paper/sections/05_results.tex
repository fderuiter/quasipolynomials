\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The UALBF computational engine is designed to seamlessly integrate
formal proofs into a continuous search pipeline, with the goal of
verifying that any quasiperfect number~$N$ must exceed a targeted bound
such as $10^{37}$ (this full computation is currently in progress).
Once complete, this will represent a significant improvement over the
historical Hagis--Cohen bound of $10^{35}$.  The verification strategy
pairs the highly parallel, lock-free Rust search engine with formally
verified constraints from Lean~4.  Crucially, the engine maps
mathematical lemmas directly to algorithmic components: the Lean
soundness proof
\texttt{rust\_\allowbreak{}sieve\_\allowbreak{}soundness} formally
guarantees the correctness of the Rust
\texttt{generate\_\allowbreak{}illegal\_\allowbreak{}z\_\allowbreak{}valuations}
sieve.  To ensure strict methodological correctness, the engine is
engineered entirely free of integer overflow bugs, utilizing up to
128-bit modular arithmetic verified via Lean FFI, and avoiding data
races through a lock-free concurrency model.
